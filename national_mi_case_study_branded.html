Case Study

Simplifying mortgage insurance
for everyone in the room

A content design overhaul that turned a technically dense lender platform into a confident, accessible experience — for both users and the business.

Company
National MI
Role
Content Designer
Domain
Mortgage Insurance Platform
Discipline
Content Design · IA · UX Writing
The challenge

Users were lost in complexity they shouldn't have to navigate

The platform helped lenders determine eligibility and calculate mortgage insurance premiums — high-stakes decisions with real regulatory weight. Research revealed three consistent friction points.

Opaque terminology
Technical mortgage jargon appeared without context, alienating users who weren't specialists in MI.
Dense, unstructured forms
Long forms with no logical grouping forced users to hunt for fields and lose their place mid-task.
Unhelpful error messages
Errors appeared without context or a clear path forward, stalling users at critical points in the flow.
The process

Research-led, systematically restructured

1
Content audit & user interviews
Catalogued all in-product language and ran structured interviews to surface where users hesitated, guessed, or gave up entirely.
Discovery
2
Restructured information architecture
Reorganised the form into four logical sections matching users' mental models — Borrower Details, Loan Information, Insurance Selection, and Quote Summary.
IA Design
3
Simplified financial terminology
Rewrote jargon-heavy labels into plain language, with contextual help text and tooltips for terms requiring deeper explanation.
UX Writing
4
Redesigned error messages
Replaced vague system errors with specific, actionable messages — what went wrong, why, and exactly how to fix it.
Error Design

Content design

Language that builds confidence, not confusion

Two areas where content design had the highest visible impact: terminology and error messages.

Before
Field label
LLPA / MI Premium Rate Factor (bps)
No explanation. No tooltip. Users guessed or called support.
Error 422: Invalid input parameters.
After
Field label
Monthly MI premium rate (?)
Tooltip provides technical detail on demand. Plain label by default.
The LTV ratio is above 95%. Lower the loan amount or increase the down payment to continue.
